Immediate Coping Strategies

1

Ground Yourself

Use the 5-4-3-2-1 technique: name 5 things you see, 4 you can touch, 3 you hear, 2 you smell, 1 you taste.

2

Breathing Exercise

Breathe in for 4 counts, hold for 4, breathe out for 6. Repeat until you feel calmer.

3

Reach Out

Call a trusted friend, family member, or use one of the crisis resources above.

4

Remove Means

If you're having thoughts of self-harm, remove or secure any means and ask someone to stay with you.

Create a Safety Plan

A safety plan is a personalized, practical plan that can help you lower your risk of future suicidal behavior.

Include:

  • • Warning signs that a crisis may be developing
  • • Coping strategies you can use on your own
  • • People and social settings that provide distraction
  • • People you can ask for help
  • • Professional agencies and clinicians to contact
  • • Ways to make your environment safe
